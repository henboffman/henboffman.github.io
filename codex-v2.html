The Monograph

A non-fiction monograph on what the documented public record of UAP / anomalous phenomena permits a careful reader to conclude, as that record exists in 2026. Six chapters, an introduction, and a methodology appendix. Every quantitative claim traces to the corpus behind the observatory.

  1. Introduction
    What this monograph contains, how the evidence base was assembled, and how the reader should approach what follows.
  2. A land under surveillance
    A 32-year arc on a single piece of land in northeastern Utah, treated as an extended case study in how institutional UAP investigation actually proceeds.
  3. The theory inventory
    The 22 interpretive theories the field has produced, ranked by how much corroborating evidence the corpus has been able to attach to each.
  4. The cases that everything turns on
    The historical cases that three or more of the field's interpretive theories invoke in common, examined as documents.
  5. The cases that fit nowhere
    The historical cases not currently invoked by any interpretive theory. Why this is, and what it tells us.
  6. The institutional response considered as its own object
    The eight-decade arc of investigative panels, debunking programs, classified contracts, and disclosure efforts, examined as a single coherent institutional behavior.
  7. What we can defensibly say in 2026
    A short final chapter on what the corpus's evidence presently permits the reader to conclude, what it does not, and what categories of further investigation would meaningfully move the field.
  8. Methodology — appendix
    How the corpus was assembled, how the support score is computed, how the orphan-case sweep was run, and how the editorial review functions. Everything needed to reproduce any claim in this monograph.

Field notes — cross-case pattern essays

  1. Field note: the EM-effects cluster
    Vehicle electrical failure, aircraft instrumentation loss, weapons-system shutdown — the documented record's electromagnetic-interference cases examined as a cluster rather than as isolated events.
  2. Field note: the water cases
    Objects observed entering, operating at, or emerging from water — the record's transmedium pattern, where every search of the water finds nothing and the absence proves nothing.
