  1. 01№
    official document

    The CIA's Durant Report on the 1953 Robertson Panel explicitly recommended that "a national policy be established as to what should be told the public regarding the phenomena, in order to minimize risk of panic" — and identified Walt Disney Productions as a potential partner for the educational program.

    The U.S. intelligence community formally proposed using the company that made *Bambi* to manage public perception of unidentified objects. Nobel laureate Luis Alvarez reviewed approximately 75 case files over four days to sign off on it.

    Source. Durant Report, declassified 1975 (CIA-RDP90-00708R000200070024-3).

    Robertson Panel (1953)
  2. 02№
    missing record

    A 1995 GAO investigation requested by Rep. Steven Schiff found that every outgoing administrative message from Roswell Army Air Field between October 1946 and December 1949 had been destroyed without authorization.

    An entire base's record of a critical period is missing. The destruction itself is documented; the contents are not recoverable.

    Source. GAO/NSIAD-95-187 (1995).

    Roswell (1947)
  3. 03№
    admitted black operation

    USAF Special Agent Richard Doty has admitted on camera that, from 1980, he fed Albuquerque physicist Paul Bennewitz fabricated documents under an AFOSI counterintelligence operation specifically intended to drive him into a paranoid breakdown.

    The "Dulce Base" mythos that propagated globally through 1990s ufology traces directly to a USAF officer admitting on film that he manufactured it. Bennewitz was institutionalised in 1988; he died in 2003.

    Source. Doty, on-camera interview, *Mirage Men* (Pilkington, 2013).

    Paul Bennewitz
  4. 04№
    named witness illness

    Betty Cash (hospitalised 15 days), Vickie Landrum, and her grandson Colby Landrum (age 7) all developed symptoms consistent with ionising radiation exposure — corneal damage, hair loss, permanent vision impairment — after a 1980 encounter outside Huffman, Texas. Their lawsuit was dismissed because no U.S. agency would acknowledge the helicopters or the object.

    A seven-year-old child with documented radiation symptoms. The court did not deny the encounter. It denied that anyone was responsible.

    Source. *Cash v. United States*, H-81-2700 (S.D. Tex., dismissed 1986).

    Cash-Landrum (1980)
  5. 05№
    named witness later killed

    In May 1996 a Russian pensioner named Tamara Prosvirina brought home a 25-centimetre humanoid corpse she found near the Mayak nuclear complex at Kyshtym. The body was filmed by Kyshtym TV. Three years after the corpse disappeared from custody, Prosvirina was struck and killed by a truck on 5 August 1999.

    The corpse was real enough to be filmed and forensically examined. The witness died after it vanished. The chain of custody on her death is not unusual; the timing is.

    Source. REN-TV broadcast (Kyshtym); Yermolayeva forensic genetic analysis (Ekaterinburg, 2004).

    Kyshtym region
  6. 06№
    unexplained physical evidence

    The original 1959 Soviet forensic radiometric reports on the Dyatlov Pass victims confirmed beta-radiation contamination on two of the nine victims' garments — Kolevatov's and Dubinina's sweaters — with no conventional explanation offered. This detail is in the original case file and has not been addressed by the 2020 avalanche-and-slab conclusion.

    Russia closed the case in 2020 with an avalanche explanation that does not deposit beta-emitting isotopes on clothing. The sixty-year-old radiological detail is still hanging.

    Source. 1959 Soviet criminal case file no. 3/2518-59, archived radiometric reports.

    Dyatlov Pass
  7. 07№
    documented suppression

    On 22 January 1958 Mike Wallace cut off NICAP director Donald Keyhoe mid-sentence on live CBS, an action CBS later acknowledged had been taken on Air Force instructions. NICAP's board at the time included Adm. Roscoe Hillenkoetter, the first Director of Central Intelligence.

    The first DCI was on the board of the civilian organisation the Air Force was actively gagging on national television. Both institutions had the same telephone numbers.

    Source. Surviving CBS tape; subsequent CBS acknowledgement to NICAP correspondence.

    Donald Keyhoe
  8. 08№
    missing record

    A 2014 Australian FOI request (FOI 064/13/14) for the Department of Air's file on the Westall 1966 schoolyard incident returned a single page stating that "no records relating to this matter can be located" — despite Australian press and the RAAF having dispatched personnel to the school the same day.

    Two hundred children, faculty, and visiting witnesses saw the object land in a field. Personnel were dispatched. Almost five decades later the file has one page in it, and the page is the denial that any file exists.

    Source. Australian Department of Defence FOI release 064/13/14 (2014).

    Westall, Melbourne
  9. 09№
    named officers killed

    The two U.S. Army Air Force intelligence officers carrying the Maury Island debris samples — Capt. William Davidson and Lt. Frank Brown — were killed when their B-25 (44-29126) crashed near Kelso, Washington on 1 August 1947. The samples were destroyed in the crash.

    A documented fatal aircraft accident, named officers, a verifiable airframe serial. The phrase "the samples were destroyed in the crash" appears in primary military records.

    Source. U.S. Army Air Force Aircraft Accident Report, 1 August 1947.

    Maury Island
  10. 10№
    unresolved prediction

    In May and November 1989 Bob Lazar described element 115 as the reactor fuel for the craft he claimed to have worked on at S-4. Element 115 was not synthesised until 2003 at JINR Dubna and was officially named Moscovium in 2016.

    The rest of Lazar's biography is in deep dispute. The atomic-number prediction sits as a separate, unresolved data point. The element exists. Its longest-lived isotope is currently measured in milliseconds.

    Source. KLAS-TV interview tapes, May and November 1989 (George Knapp); IUPAC, 2016.

    Bob Lazar
  11. 11№
    official document

    Lt. Col. Charles Halt's 13 January 1981 official USAF memo to the UK Ministry of Defence — released under FOIA in 1983 — formally documents radiation readings approximately ten times background at three indentations in the forest floor, with the peak readings taken at the indentations themselves (0.05–0.07 mR).

    U.S. Air Force letterhead. A lieutenant colonel's signature. Specific microroentgen readings tied to specific ground locations. Filed inside the chain of command, not leaked.

    Source. Halt Memo, 13 Jan 1981, declassified via FOIA 1983.

    Rendlesham (1980)
  12. 12№
    federal compensation

    A1C John Burroughs, one of the Rendlesham 1980 witnesses, was granted 100% disability by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs in 2014 — the first VA disability award explicitly tied to a UFO encounter, citing cardiac and neurological damage.

    A federal benefits agency formally compensated a service member for injuries sustained during an event that the same government officially says did not happen.

    Source. VA disability determination, 2014; Burroughs FOIA correspondence.

    Rendlesham (1980)
  13. 13№
    unexplained physical evidence

    Russian drilling at the Kola Superdeep Borehole recovered microscopic plankton fossils dated to roughly 2 billion years from 6.7 km depth — and free liquid water in rock cracks at depths where the rock should have been impermeable.

    The deepest hole ever drilled returned biological material older than complex cellular life is supposed to exist, in a place where water is not supposed to be possible.

    Source. Kozlovsky (ed.), *The Superdeep Well of the Kola Peninsula*, Springer 1987.

    Kola Superdeep Borehole
  14. 14№
    isolated chemistry

    Subglacial Lake Vostok holds water supersaturated with oxygen and nitrogen at roughly fifty times the concentration of any surface lake on Earth, sustained by 360 atmospheres of pressure under 3,750 metres of ice. The 2012 Russian drilling breakthrough used kerosene and Freon as antifreeze; biologists protested the contamination risk for years before it was permitted.

    An isolated body of water the size of Lake Ontario, sealed for around 15 million years, breached using industrial solvents over international scientific objection.

    Source. *Nature*, 8 Feb 2012; Bulat et al., subsequent reports.

    Vostok Station
  15. 15№
    audio in evidence

    At 19:12:28 on 21 October 1978 the Melbourne Flight Service Unit recorded 17 seconds of metallic scraping immediately after pilot Frederick Valentich's transmission "It's not an aircraft," then loss of signal. No wreckage of his Cessna 182L was ever recovered despite a major RAAF and RAN search. The Australian Department of Transport's 1982 final report concluded "not determined."

    The tape exists and is in the public domain. The seventeen seconds of metallic sound is on the recording. No aircraft fragment, no body, no fuel slick was ever found.

    Source. Australian Dept. of Transport Aircraft Accident Investigation Report, 27 April 1982.

    Frederick Valentich
  16. 16№
    evidence above the explanation

    Marfa Army Air Field operated 1942–1947, training pilots in twin-engine instrument flight. Pilots based at Marfa AAF independently recorded sightings of the Marfa Lights in their flight logs — observations made above the local atmospheric inversion layer, undermining the highway-mirage hypothesis.

    The leading scientific explanation for the Marfa Lights is refracted highway traffic. The U.S. military's own pilots wrote down sightings made above the layer that does the refracting.

    Source. Marfa AAF operational records, NARA RG 18; Stephens, *The Marfa Lights*, 2003.

    Marfa
  17. 17№

    Governor Fife Symington III publicly ridiculed the March 1997 Phoenix Lights sightings at the time — staging a press conference with an aide dressed in an alien costume. In a 2007 *Daily Courier* op-ed he wrote that he himself had personally witnessed the V-formation craft above his property.

    A sitting governor mocked thousands of his own constituents on camera while concealing that he was one of them.

    Source. *Daily Courier* (Prescott, AZ), 18 March 2007.

    Phoenix Lights (1997)
  18. 18№
    instrument failure pattern

    Two Iranian Imperial Air Force F-4 Phantoms scrambled to intercept a luminous object over Tehran on 19 September 1976 each suffered simultaneous loss of weapons systems, avionics, and radio communications on close approach. Both aircraft regained all systems on egress. The incident is documented in a 4-page DIA report formally distributed to the White House, CIA, NSA, and Joint Chiefs.

    Two military jets crewed by named pilots lost every electronic system on approach to the same object and recovered them all on retreat. The DIA writeup is one of the cleanest documents in the public record on instrumentation effects.

    Source. Defense Intelligence Agency report DIA-2540-1B-78 (1978).

    Tehran F-4 (1976)
