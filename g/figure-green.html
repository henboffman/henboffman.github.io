Christopher "Kit" Green — former CIA Office of Scientific Intelligence senior officer; medical doctor (Wayne State neurology); participated in the AAWSAP-era Bigelow "Discovery Team"; one of the small handful of named senior intelligence figures with on-record interest in UAP-related medical effects.

What's documented

Christopher “Kit” Green, MD PhD. CIA Office of Scientific Intelligence senior analyst 1969–1985; Assistant National Intelligence Officer for Science and Technology. Later General Motors chief technology officer for biomedical research; professor of neurology at Wayne State University School of Medicine. Green’s CIA role included medical-intelligence assessments of foreign biological and psychotronic-weapons programs, and during the Stargate years he was the agency liaison for remote-viewing review. He is named in Skinwalkers at the Pentagon (Lacatski, Kelleher, Knapp, 2021) as a member of the small AAWSAP-era “Discovery Team” at Bigelow Aerospace. Green has spoken publicly (in academic conference talks and Pasulka’s American Cosmic) about evaluating ~125 cases of putative UAP-related medical injuries — burns, radiation-pattern effects, neurological sequelae — over a forty-year career. The interview corpus is one of the few non-anecdotal medical datasets in the public-facing UAP discourse.
