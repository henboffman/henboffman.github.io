FigureTravis S. Taylor (b. 1968)

Aerospace engineer and physicist; lead investigator on The Secret of Skinwalker Ranch (S1–present); former DoD directed-energy contractor.

What's documented

PhD physics, University of Alabama in Huntsville; multiple engineering advanced degrees. Long career as a Department of Defense contractor working on directed-energy, missile-defense, and advanced-propulsion research at Marshall Space Flight Center and through various aerospace subcontractors. Author of multiple hard-science-fiction novels (including the Tau Ceti Agenda series) and non-fiction works on space and physics for general audiences.

Joined the Skinwalker Ranch investigation in 2020 as the show’s central interpretive voice. Travis’s role on-screen is to translate instrument readings into engineer’s plain-spoken claims about what the data ‘means’ — a function that has made him both the show’s most quoted figure and its most contested one. Notable claims he has made on-air include a ‘radiation attack’ incident (S1E6), the soil-conductivity result (S2E8), and the high-zone stimulus-response UAP interpretation (S2E9).

Notable & intriguing

  • First major on-air 'radiation attack' claim (S1E6 'Poking the Nest'), framed as evidence the ranch responds to intrusive investigation.

  • Lead author of the show's 'methodology as discovery' framing — each season's equipment additions are introduced primarily through his on-camera explanations.
