IncidentLevelland, Texas EM Effects Wave (2–3 November 1957)

Over a six-hour period on the night of 2–3 November 1957 in and around Levelland, Texas, at least fifteen independent witnesses — including the local sheriff and a fire marshal — reported a glowing egg-shaped object whose approach caused vehicle engines and headlights to fail.

What's documented

Beginning at approximately 22:50 on 2 November 1957, Hockley County Sheriff Weir Clem began receiving calls from motorists reporting their cars had stalled and their headlights gone dark in the presence of a luminous oblong object on or near the road. The calls came from a 20-mile radius around the small West Texas town of Levelland. Sheriff Clem, deputy Pat McCulloch, fire marshal Ray Jones, and Levelland patrolman A.J. Fowler all independently observed the object during the night. Witnesses included truck driver Pedro Saucedo (the first reported call, 22:30), college student Newell Wright, and farm worker José Alvarez. Project Blue Book sent a single investigator, Sgt. Norman P. Barth, who spent approximately seven hours in the area on 5 November and interviewed three witnesses. Blue Book’s official conclusion was “ball lightning” — despite the fact that the official Air Weather Service record for the night showed clear skies, no thunderstorm activity, and no precipitation within several hundred miles. The Levelland case is the most-cited single-night EM-effects cluster in Blue Book’s files.

Notable & intriguing

  • At least fifteen independent witnesses across a 20-mile radius around Levelland, Texas, on the night of 2–3 November 1957, reported engine and headlight failure in the presence of a luminous oblong object; recoveries of vehicle function were reported after the object departed.

    Project Blue Book case 5347; Hockley County Sheriff's Department log, 2–3 November 1957; Donald Keyhoe, *Flying Saucers — Top Secret*, 1960

  • Hockley County Sheriff Weir Clem personally observed the object at approximately 01:30 on 3 November 1957; his report describes 'a brilliant red object… shaped like a football… about the size of a baseball at arm's length, gliding silently across the highway 300 feet ahead.'

    Sheriff Weir Clem signed statement, 3 November 1957, Project Blue Book case file

  • Project Blue Book's official 'ball lightning' attribution was contradicted by the contemporaneous Air Weather Service record, which showed clear skies and no electrical-storm activity within several hundred miles on the night in question; J. Allen Hynek, then Blue Book's scientific consultant, later cited Levelland as one of the cases that prompted his 1969 break with the program.

    Air Weather Service surface analyses, 2–3 November 1957; J. Allen Hynek, *The UFO Experience*, Henry Regnery, 1972, ch. 8
