IncidentStephenville, Texas (8 January 2008)

Multiple witnesses including a pilot, a constable, and a local businessman reported a large, silent, low-flying object over Stephenville and Dublin, Texas; FAA radar data later confirmed the presence of an unidentified track over the Bush ranch.

What's documented

At approximately 18:15 local time on 8 January 2008, residents of Stephenville and the surrounding Erath County reported a large, silent object with bright lights flying low over the area. Witnesses included Steve Allen, a private pilot and freight company owner; Erath County constable Lee Roy Gaitan; and Stephenville businessman Ricky Sorrells. The Air Force initially denied any aircraft activity in the area, then on 23 January 2008 reversed and stated that ten F-16s from the 457th Fighter Squadron at Naval Air Station Fort Worth Joint Reserve Base had been conducting training exercises that night. MUFON investigator Robert Powell obtained FAA radar data via FOIA showing an unidentified track moving toward the Western White House (the Bush ranch in Crawford, Texas) at high speed without a transponder return.

Notable & intriguing

  • MUFON FOIA request to the FAA returned radar data showing an unidentified, transponderless target moving at speeds up to 2,100 mph on a vector toward the Crawford, Texas presidential ranch on the night of 8 January 2008.

    FAA radar data, MUFON FOIA case, 2008; analysis by Robert Powell, *Special Research Report: Stephenville Lights*, MUFON, July 2008

  • The 301st Fighter Wing public affairs office initially denied any military aircraft activity in the Stephenville area on the night of 8 January 2008; 15 days later, on 23 January 2008, the office reversed its statement and confirmed that ten F-16s from the 457th Fighter Squadron had been conducting training exercises that night.

    Maj. Karl Lewis, 301st FW PAO, original statement 11 January 2008 and revised statement 23 January 2008; Stephenville Empire-Tribune

  • Witnesses including Erath County constable Lee Roy Gaitan and Stephenville Lions Club pilot Steve Allen reported the object as silent, larger than a Boeing 747, and traveling at altitudes as low as 3,000 feet.

    Witness statements collected by MUFON Texas, January 2008; CNN Larry King Live, 18 January 2008
