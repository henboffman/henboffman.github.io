I'm Not Like You

The documented record of unidentified aerial phenomena — indexed as one graph, reported as one monograph.

Ways in

  1. Read the monograph Six chapters on what the public record permits a careful reader to conclude — readable end-to-end in about an hour — plus the orphan-case briefs and the field-note essays on cross-case patterns (the EM-effects cluster, the water cases). Every number traces to the observatory.
  2. Read the deep dives Case studies in multi-source structured depth: Skinwalker Ranch (a 32-year arc, every episode of the show indexed), the Nimitz Tic-Tac, Rendlesham Forest, and Cash-Landrum — timeline, personnel, sensor record, images, and every conventional explanation with the corpus's stance on it.
  3. Open the constellation Every place, figure, case, phenomenon, and interpretive theory as one graph. Drag to pan, scroll to zoom, click any node to enter the underlying material. Filter by type. The shape of the discourse, visible.
  4. Browse the index Real places (Roswell, Rendlesham, Kola, Vostok, Dyatlov Pass…), figures, cases, and ongoing phenomena, organized by category. Photo grid + bucketed lists.
  5. The creepy index Hand-curated rank-order of the items in the public record that don't get easier with re-reading. Three windows: historical (pre-1990), 1990-2015, post-2015. Same sourcing standard across all three.
  6. Theories The interpretive frames that have been advanced for the material — extraterrestrial, interdimensional, psychosocial, ancient-astronaut, counterintelligence-product, simulation, and others. Each frame names its proponents and the cases its proponents adduce as evidence.
  7. Suggested watching Documentaries, films, and series that cover the documented record. De-duped alphabetical catalog with backlinks: Mirage Men, The Phenomenon, Hunt for the Skinwalker, the Halt interviews, the C-SPAN Grusch hearing — the essentials are flagged.

Every entry is documented public record. Named witnesses, court cases, declassified memos, FOIA returns, peer-reviewed papers, federal accident reports. Working draft. The graph keeps growing.
