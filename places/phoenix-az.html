What's documented

Phoenix is the capital of Arizona. On the evening of 13 March 1997, a series of widely witnessed luminous phenomena moved across Arizona from the Nevada line to the Mexican border. Two distinct event sequences were involved: a triangular formation of lights moving slowly south, and a separate set of stationary lights hovering over the Estrella Mountains southwest of Phoenix around 10:00 PM. Witnesses included Arizona Governor Fife Symington, who held a press conference mocking the event before acknowledging, in 2007, that he had personally witnessed it.

Notable & intriguing

  • Arizona Governor Fife Symington III held a press conference on 19 June 1997 at which an aide dressed in an alien costume was 'arrested' for the Phoenix Lights, ridiculing eyewitness accounts. On 18 March 2007, Symington wrote in the Daily Courier that he had been a witness himself: 'I'm a pilot and I know just about every machine that flies. It was bigger than anything that I've ever seen. It remains a great mystery.'

    Daily Courier (Prescott, AZ), 18 March 2007, op-ed by Fife Symington

  • The U.S. Air Force in July 1997 attributed one of the two Phoenix Lights events to A-10 Warthogs of the Maryland Air National Guard dropping flares over the Goldwater Range during Operation Snowbird. The flare explanation has been confirmed for the 10 PM stationary lights; the earlier triangular formation is unexplained.

    USAF Public Affairs statement, July 1997; Luke AFB Operation Snowbird flight log

  • Air-traffic control radar at Luke AFB did not detect the formation. The Western Air Defense Sector's NORAD logs for the evening were not retained, contrary to standing record-retention policy.

    FOIA response to researcher Richard Motzer, 1999
