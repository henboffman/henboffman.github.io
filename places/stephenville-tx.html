Stephenville, Texas

32°13′N, 98°12′W — Erath County, North Central Texas — small city southwest of Fort Worth

32.2167, -98.2000 · view on OpenStreetMap →

What's documented

Stephenville is a small city in Erath County, Texas, population ~21,000. On the evening of 8 January 2008, multiple residents reported a very large luminous object moving silently over the area at low altitude. Witnesses included a pilot, a police officer, a city constable, and the owner of a local machine shop. MUFON investigated the case; in subsequent FAA radar analysis, researcher Glen Schulze and journalist Robert Powell identified anomalous radar returns over the area on the evening in question.

Notable & intriguing

  • MUFON's Glen Schulze and Robert Powell obtained FAA radar data via FOIA for the 8 January 2008 evening and identified an unidentified track moving at 532 mph toward Crawford, Texas — at that date, the location of President George W. Bush's Prairie Chapel Ranch. The object passed within 10 miles of the ranch at 8:21 PM Central.

    Schulze & Powell, 'Special Research Report: Stephenville, Texas' (MUFON, 2008)

  • On 23 January 2008, the U.S. Air Force's 301st Fighter Wing at NAS Fort Worth Joint Reserve Base reversed its initial statement that no military aircraft had been over Erath County on 8 January, and acknowledged that ten F-16s from the 457th Fighter Squadron had in fact been operating training flights over the area.

    Stephenville Empire-Tribune, 23 January 2008

  • Erath County Constable Lee Roy Gaitan provided sworn testimony to MUFON that the object he observed was approximately a half-mile long, silent, with flashing strobe-like white lights covering its underside.

    MUFON case file 14159, January 2008
